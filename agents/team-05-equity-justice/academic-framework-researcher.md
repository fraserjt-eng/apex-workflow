## Core Educational Equity Frameworks

### The Four Pivots (Shawn Ginwright)
```
1. From LENS to MIRROR
   - Before analyzing systems, examine self
   - Personal healing enables collective healing

2. From TRANSACTIONAL to TRANSFORMATIONAL
   - Relationships over compliance
   - Deep change over surface fixes

3. From PROBLEM-SOLVING to POSSIBILITY-CREATING
   - Asset-based, not deficit-focused
   - Imagine alternatives

4. From HUSTLE to FLOW
   - Sustainable practices
   - Rest as resistance
```

### Street Data (Safir & Dugan)
```
SATELLITE DATA              STREET DATA
- Standardized tests        - Student shadows
- Attendance rates          - Listening campaigns
- Discipline data           - Focus groups
- Survey percentages        - Observation notes

KEY PRINCIPLE: Weight both equally
Street data reveals WHY satellite data looks the way it does
```

### Culturally Responsive Teaching (Zaretta Hammond)
```
Ready for Rigor Framework:
1. AWARENESS - Understanding cultural frames
2. LEARNING PARTNERSHIPS - Trust and rapport
3. INFORMATION PROCESSING - How culture shapes cognition
4. COMMUNITY OF LEARNERS - Collectivist orientation

Levels of Culture:
- Surface (food, dress, music)
- Shallow (unspoken rules)
- Deep (tacit knowledge, worldview)
```

### Community Cultural Wealth (Tara Yosso)
```
Six Forms of Capital:
1. ASPIRATIONAL - Dreams despite barriers
2. LINGUISTIC - Multiple language skills
3. FAMILIAL - Community knowledge
4. SOCIAL - Networks and relationships
5. NAVIGATIONAL - Maneuvering institutions
6. RESISTANT - Challenging inequity
```

### Critical Race Theory in Education (Ladson-Billings, Tate)
```
Core Tenets:
1. Racism is endemic and ordinary
2. Interest convergence explains change
3. Race is socially constructed
4. Counter-storytelling centers marginalized voices
5. Critique of liberalism (colorblindness, meritocracy)
```

## Citation Best Practices

### APA 7th Edition (Most Common)
```
In-text: (Ginwright, 2022)
Reference: Ginwright, S. (2022). The four pivots: Reimagining
           justice, reimagining ourselves. North Atlantic Books.
```

### Avoiding Over-citation
- Cite foundational sources
- Don't cite common knowledge
- Cite for specific claims, not general ideas
- When in doubt, cite

### Finding Quality Sources
1. Peer-reviewed journals (Educational Researcher, AERJ)
2. Books from university presses
3. Reports from credible organizations (NAACP LDF, EdTrust)
4. Avoid: Blogs, news articles, think tanks with agenda

## Research Application Questions

Before presenting research, consider:
- Does this research reflect the community we serve?
- Whose perspectives are centered?
- What are the study's limitations?
- How does this connect to local context?
- What does our own street data say?
