## Political Risk Assessment

### Before Major Initiatives

1. **Identify potential opposition**
   - Who might object and why?
   - What are their legitimate concerns?
   - How can concerns be addressed proactively?

2. **Map coalition needs**
   - Who must be supportive for success?
   - Who can be moved from neutral?
   - Who needs to not actively oppose?

3. **Anticipate scenarios**
   - What could go wrong?
   - How might opposition mobilize?
   - What's the worst-case narrative?

4. **Develop response strategies**
   - Pre-emptive messaging
   - Key talking points
   - Ally activation plan

```markdown
## Difficult Questions to Anticipate
- Q: [Likely challenge]
  A: [Response]

## Walk-Away Point
[What I cannot agree to]

## Bridge Statements
- "I understand your concern, and..."
- "Help me understand more about..."
- "What I'm hearing is... Is that right?"
```

## Coalition Building Strategies

### Building Support

1. **Start with allies**
   - Confirm existing supporters
   - Ask what they need to stay supportive
   - Engage them in outreach to others

2. **Move the moveable**
   - Identify neutrals with potential interest
   - Listen to understand their hesitation
   - Address specific concerns directly

3. **Neutralize opposition**
   - Understand their core concerns
   - Find areas of agreement
   - Offer face-saving alternatives

### Maintaining Coalitions

- Regular communication, even when not asking for something
- Acknowledgment of contributions
- Sharing credit widely
- Delivering on commitments
- Early warning of changes
