## Equity Audit Framework

### Five Domains of Educational Equity

1. **Access Equity**
   - Who has access to what programs, courses, resources?
   - Gifted/AP/IB enrollment vs. demographics
   - Extracurricular participation
   - Technology and material access

2. **Achievement Equity**
   - Outcome patterns by student group
   - Proficiency rates disaggregated
   - Growth patterns for all students
   - Graduation and credit accrual

3. **Discipline Equity**
   - Referral patterns by group
   - Suspension/expulsion rates
   - Type of consequence by group
   - Staff reporting patterns

4. **Climate Equity**
   - Experience of belonging by group
   - Perceived safety and respect
   - Voice and representation
   - Staff-student relationships

5. **Workforce Equity**
   - Staff diversity representation
   - Leadership diversity
   - Hiring and retention patterns
   - Professional development access

## Disproportionality Analysis

### Key Metrics

**Risk Ratio**
```
Risk Ratio = (% of Group X in outcome) / (% of Group X in population)

Example:
- Black students = 30% of population
- Black students = 60% of suspensions
- Risk Ratio = 60/30 = 2.0 (2x overrepresented)
```

**Composition Index**
```
Composition Index = (Group % in outcome) / (Group % in population)

Interpretation:
- 1.0 = Proportional representation
- >1.0 = Overrepresented
- <1.0 = Underrepresented
```

### Red Flag Thresholds
| Metric | Yellow Flag | Red Flag |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Risk Ratio | 1.5 - 2.0 | >2.0 |
| Discipline Gap | 5-10 pts | >10 pts |
| Access Gap | 10-15 pts | >15 pts |

## Audit Process

### Phase 1: Data Collection
1. Gather quantitative data (3 years minimum)
2. Disaggregate by required groups
3. Calculate disproportionality metrics
4. Identify patterns and trends

### Phase 2: Context & Voice
1. Review policies and procedures
2. Gather stakeholder perspectives
3. Conduct focus groups (students, families, staff)
4. Observe practices in action

### Phase 3: Root Cause Analysis
1. Identify potential contributing factors
2. Distinguish symptoms from causes
3. Map systemic patterns
4. Acknowledge historical context

### Phase 4: Recommendations
1. Develop specific, actionable recommendations
2. Prioritize by impact and feasibility
3. Identify quick wins and long-term changes
4. Resource implications

### Phase 5: Action Planning
1. Set measurable goals
2. Assign ownership
3. Create timeline
4. Establish progress monitoring

## Justice-Centered Approach

### What Makes This Different

**Traditional Audit:**
- Focuses on compliance
- Identifies "problem" groups
- Recommends fixes for students

**Justice-Centered Audit:**
- Focuses on liberation
- Identifies systemic barriers
- Recommends changes to systems
- Centers impacted voices
- Names historical context
- Challenges root causes

### Essential Questions
- What systems produce these outcomes?
- Who benefits from current arrangements?
- Whose voices are missing from decisions?
- What would change if those most impacted led?
- What are we willing to give up for equity?

## Common Resistance Patterns

| Resistance | Translation | Response |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| "It's about behavior, not race" | Denial of pattern | Show data, explore root causes |
| "We treat everyone the same" | Color-blind ideology | Discuss equity vs. equality |
| "Parents don't value education" | Deficit thinking | Center family voice, examine access |
| "We've tried everything" | Helplessness | Examine what hasn't been tried |
| "It's just a few bad actors" | Individualization | Show systemic patterns |
