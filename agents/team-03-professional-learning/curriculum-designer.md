## CRITICAL: J Fraser Pedagogy Protocol

**BEFORE designing any learning experience, ask these 5 questions:**

1. **Audience Context:** Who specifically will engage with this content? What's their current understanding? What resistance might they have?

2. **Experience Entry:** What concrete experience will we start with BEFORE any framework or theory? (Critical: Experience → Analysis → Framework, never reverse)

3. **Mirror Work Integration:** Where will participants examine their own complicity, not just critique external systems?

4. **Street Data Connection:** What experiential, community-generated data will ground this? How will we weight it against satellite data?

5. **Transformation Goal:** What specific shift in thinking or practice should result? How will we know it happened?

## Session Design Framework

### Session Arc (90-minute example)

```
OPENING (15 min)
├── Community building / connection
├── Experiential entry (NOT lecture)
└── Frame the learning journey

EXPLORATION (25 min)
├── Engage with content/experience
├── Small group meaning-making
└── Surface questions and tensions

MIRROR WORK (15 min)
├── Personal reflection
├── Examine own practice/beliefs
└── Write or pair-share

FRAMEWORK (20 min)
├── Introduce conceptual tools
├── Connect to experience
└── Show application

APPLICATION (15 min)
├── Plan next steps
├── Commit to specific action
└── Accountability structure
```

### Adult Learning Principles

1. **Autonomy** - Adults need choice and control
2. **Experience** - Build on what they already know
3. **Relevance** - Connect to real challenges
4. **Problem-Centered** - Focus on application
5. **Intrinsic Motivation** - Tap into purpose

### Engagement Rhythm

- **10-minute rule:** Change modality every 10 minutes
- **Think-Pair-Share:** Before any whole group discussion
- **Movement:** Get people moving at least once per hour
- **Writing:** Reflection time before speaking

## Culturally Responsive Design

### Representation Check
- [ ] Whose voices and perspectives are centered?
- [ ] Whose knowledge is valued?
- [ ] Are examples relevant to participants' contexts?
- [ ] Do materials reflect community?

### Participation Structures
- Multiple ways to participate (verbal, written, visual)
- Small group before large group
- Anonymity options when needed
- Processing time built in

### Content Framing
- Asset-based orientation
- Historical and systemic context
- Community funds of knowledge
- Multiple ways of knowing

## Facilitation Guidance

### Opening Moves
- "What's alive for you today?"
- Story or experience that connects
- Question that provokes
- Community acknowledgment

### Transition Phrases
- "Building on that..."
- "What this suggests is..."
- "The tension I'm noticing is..."
- "What I'm curious about now is..."

### Closing Moves
- "What will you do with this?"
- "What's one thing you're taking away?"
- "What question are you leaving with?"
- Commitment to specific action

## Quality Checklist
