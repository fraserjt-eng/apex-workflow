## Coaching Philosophy

### High Warmth + High Structure

**High Warmth:**
- Deep belief in leader's capacity
- Genuine care for their wellbeing
- Attentive listening
- Celebration of growth
- Safe space for vulnerability

**High Structure:**
- Clear expectations
- Direct feedback
- Accountability for commitments
- Progress monitoring
- Non-negotiables named

### Coaching Stance Continuum

```
CONSULTING ←------------------------→ COACHING
(Providing answers)           (Facilitating discovery)

Use based on:
- Urgency of situation
- Coachee's capacity
- Trust relationship
- Type of challenge (technical vs. adaptive)
```

## Coaching Conversation Structures

### Reflective Coaching Protocol

```
1. CHECK-IN (5 min)
   - How are you? (personal connection)
   - What's on your mind?

2. FOCUS (5 min)
   - What would be most useful to think about today?
   - What outcome do you want from our conversation?

3. EXPLORATION (20 min)
   - What's the current situation?
   - What have you tried?
   - What's the underlying challenge?
   - What are you noticing about yourself?

4. POSSIBILITIES (10 min)
   - What options do you see?
   - What else might be possible?
   - What would [admired leader] do?
   - What's the courageous choice?

5. COMMITMENT (10 min)
   - What will you do?
   - By when?
   - How will you know it worked?
   - What support do you need?
```

### Feedback Conversation Protocol

```
1. SETUP (2 min)
   - State purpose clearly
   - Frame as development-focused

2. OBSERVATION (5 min)
   - What I saw/heard (specific, behavioral)
   - No interpretation yet

3. IMPACT (5 min)
   - What effect this had
   - Why it matters

4. INQUIRY (10 min)
   - What was your thinking?
   - What did you notice?
   - What would you do differently?

5. FORWARD (5 min)
   - What will you try next?
   - What support do you need?
   - When will we follow up?
```

### Difficult Conversation Protocol

```
1. GROUNDING
   - Name the purpose directly
   - Acknowledge difficulty
   - Affirm relationship and intent

2. DESCRIBE
   - Specific behaviors/situations
   - Impact on students/staff/community
   - Pattern if applicable

3. LISTEN
   - Their perspective fully
   - Don't interrupt to correct
   - Paraphrase understanding

4. EXPECTATIONS
   - Clear statement of what must change
   - Timeline for change
   - Consequences if unchanged

5. SUPPORT
   - What you will provide
   - Resources available
   - Follow-up schedule

6. COMMITMENT
   - Their words on what they'll do
   - Written if formal
   - Next check-in scheduled
```

## Powerful Coaching Questions

### Opening Questions
- "What's keeping you up at night?"
- "Where are you feeling most stuck?"
- "What's the real challenge here for you?"

### Deepening Questions
- "What are you afraid might happen?"
- "What would you do if you weren't afraid?"
- "What's the pattern you're noticing?"
- "How might you be contributing to this?"

### Possibility Questions
- "What would 'great' look like?"
- "Who does this well that you could learn from?"
- "What's one small experiment you could try?"
- "What would you tell a friend in this situation?"

### Accountability Questions
- "What will you actually do?"
- "What might get in the way?"
- "How will we know it's working?"
- "What do you need from me?"

## Leadership Development Pathways

### First-Year Principal Support
- Weekly coaching calls
- Monthly walkthrough with debrief
- Peer cohort connection
- Emergency support available

### Developing Leader Support
- Bi-weekly coaching calls
- Quarterly deep-dive observations
- Action research project
- Leadership shadowing opportunities

### Experienced Leader Support
- Monthly coaching calls
- Peer coaching pairs
- Mentoring new leaders
- Innovation projects

## Mirror Work for Leaders

Essential self-reflection areas:
- How do I respond to resistance?
- What triggers my defensiveness?
- Where do I avoid difficult conversations?
- How do my identities shape my leadership?
- What am I not seeing in my building?
- Where am I complicit in inequity?
