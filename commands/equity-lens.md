# APEX Equity Lens Analysis

You are now applying an equity lens to the content provided.

**Content to Analyze:** $ARGUMENTS

## Equity Analysis Framework

### 1. Access Analysis

**Questions:**
- Who has access to this? Who doesn't?
- What barriers exist for different groups?
- Who decides access?
- Is access equitable or just equal?

**Look for:**
- Assumptions about resources, technology, time
- Language accessibility
- Cultural accessibility
- Physical accessibility

### 2. Impact Analysis

**Questions:**
- Who benefits from this? How?
- Who might be harmed? How?
- Are impacts different for different groups?
- Who bears the burden of change?

**Look for:**
- Differential outcomes by group
- Unintended consequences
- Power dynamics
- Historical context

### 3. Voice Analysis

**Questions:**
- Whose voice is centered? Missing?
- Who made decisions? Who was consulted?
- Whose expertise is valued?
- Who can give feedback?

**Look for:**
- Representation in development
- Community involvement
- Student and family voice
- Staff voice

### 4. Power Analysis

**Questions:**
- Who has power in this situation?
- Who gains/loses power?
- What systems are reinforced?
- What could shift power?

**Look for:**
- Decision-making authority
- Resource control
- Whose comfort is centered
- Historical power patterns

### 5. Language Analysis

**Questions:**
- Is language asset-based or deficit-based?
- Is language accessible?
- Does language center dominant culture?
- Are terms defined/explained?

**Deficit language flags:**
- "At-risk" → "historically marginalized"
- "Achievement gap" → "opportunity gap"
- "Low-performing" → "under-resourced"
- "Minority" → "historically excluded"
- "Disadvantaged" → "underserved"
